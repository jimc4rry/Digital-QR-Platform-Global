{% trans "What drives the cost of a printed menu" %}

{% blocktrans %}A printed menu looks cheaper upfront, but the real cost shows up over time: a price change, a seasonal dish, or a supplier shortage means a reprint - design time, printing costs, and menus that are wrong until the new batch arrives. Multiply that by however many times a year your prices or dishes change.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "What you're actually paying for" %}

{% blocktrans %}The subscription isn't just hosting a PDF online. It covers the QR code generation, unlimited menu edits with no reprinting cost, dietary labels, and - on the higher plans - order management and sales reporting that a printed menu simply can't provide.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Which plan should you pick?" %}

{% blocktrans %}If you just want to replace a printed menu with something always up to date, Basic covers it. If customers should be able to order directly from the table without waving down a server, Pro is the natural fit. If you're running a busier operation with staff accounts and want to see which dishes actually sell, Business adds the reporting layer on top.{% endblocktrans %}
